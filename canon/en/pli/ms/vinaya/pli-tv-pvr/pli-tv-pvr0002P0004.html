2.4 Saṅgahavāra
The classes of offenses in each offense

Avassutāya bhikkhuniyā avassutassa purisapuggalassa kāyasaṁsaggaṁ sādiyantiyā āpattiyo sattannaṁ āpattikkhandhānaṁ katihi āpattikkhandhehi saṅgahitā?
When it comes to the offenses for a lustful nun consenting to a lustful man making physical contact with her, in how many of the seven classes of offenses are they found?

Avassutāya bhikkhuniyā avassutassa purisapuggalassa kāyasaṁsaggaṁ sādiyantiyā āpattiyo sattannaṁ āpattikkhandhānaṁ tīhi āpattikkhandhehi saṅgahitā—
They are found in three:

siyā pārājikāpattikkhandhena, siyā thullaccayāpattikkhandhena, siyā dukkaṭāpattikkhandhena …pe….
they may be in the class of offenses entailing expulsion; they may be in the class of serious offenses; they may be in the class of offenses of wrong conduct. …

Dadhiṁ viññāpetvā bhuñjantiyā āpattiyo sattannaṁ āpattikkhandhānaṁ katihi āpattikkhandhehi saṅgahitā?
When it comes to the offenses for asking for curd and then eating it, in how many of the seven classes of offenses are they found?

Dadhiṁ viññāpetvā bhuñjantiyā āpattiyo sattannaṁ āpattikkhandhānaṁ dvīhi āpattikkhandhehi saṅgahitā—
They are found in two:

siyā pāṭidesanīyāpattikkhandhena, siyā dukkaṭāpattikkhandhena.
they may be in the class of offenses entailing acknowledgment; they may be in the class of offenses of wrong conduct. …

Saṅgahavāro niṭṭhito catuttho.
The classes of offenses in each offense, the fourth, are finished.
