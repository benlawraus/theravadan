Saṁyutta Nikāya 1.18 Linked Discourses 1.18

2. Nandanavagga 2. The Garden of Delight

Hirīsutta Conscience A Sense of Shame

“Hirīnisedho puriso, “Can a person constrained by conscience “A person restrained by a sense of shame:

koci lokasmiṁ vijjati; be found in the world? Is there in the world to be found?

Yo nindaṁ apabodhati, Who shies away from blame, One who draws back from blame

asso bhadro kasāmivā”ti. like a fine horse from the whip?” As a good horse does from the whip?”

“Hirīnisedhā tanuyā, “Few are those constrained by conscience, “Few are those restrained by a sense of shame

ye caranti sadā satā; who live always mindful. Who fare always mindful;

Antaṁ dukkhassa pappuyya, Having reached the end of suffering, Few, having reached the end of suffering,

caranti visame saman”ti. smoothly they walk in the rough.” Fare evenly amidst the uneven. ”
