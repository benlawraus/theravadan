Itivuttaka 55 So It Was Said 55

Tikanipāta The Book of the Threes

Paṭhamavagga Chapter One

Dutiyaesanāsutta Searches (2nd)

Vuttañhetaṁ bhagavatā vuttamarahatāti me sutaṁ: This was said by the Buddha, the Perfected One: that is what I heard.

“Tisso imā, bhikkhave, esanā. “Mendicants, there are these three searches.

Katamā tisso? What three?

Kāmesanā, bhavesanā, brahmacariyesanā—The search for sensual pleasures, the search for continued existence, and the search for a spiritual path.

imā kho, bhikkhave, tisso esanā”ti. These are the three searches.”

Etamatthaṁ bhagavā avoca. The Buddha spoke this matter.

Tatthetaṁ iti vuccati: On this it is said:

“Kāmesanā bhavesanā, “The search for sensual pleasures, for a good rebirth,

brahmacariyesanā saha; and the search for a spiritual path;

Iti saccaparāmāso, the holding tight to the thought, ‘this is the truth’,

diṭṭhiṭṭhānā samussayā. and the mass of grounds for views—

Sabbarāgavirattassa, for one detached from all lusts,

taṇhakkhayavimuttino; freed by the ending of craving,

Esanā paṭinissaṭṭhā, that searching has been relinquished,

diṭṭhiṭṭhānā samūhatā; and those viewpoints eradicated.

Esanānaṁ khayā bhikkhu, With the ending of searches, a mendicant

nirāso akathaṅkathī”ti. is free of hope and indecision.”

Ayampi attho vutto bhagavatā, iti me sutanti. This too is a matter that was spoken by the Blessed One: that is what I heard.

Chaṭṭhaṁ.
