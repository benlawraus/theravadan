Jātaka Stories of Past Lives 159

Dukanipāta The Book of the Twos

Daḷhavagga The Chapter on the Strong

9. Morajātaka The Peacock: a past life story

“Udetayañ cakkhumā ekarājā, This Clear-eyed One arises, the One King,

Harissavaṇṇo pathavippabhāso; palomino-colored, shining over the earth.

Taṁ taṁ namassāmi harissavaṇṇaṁ pathavippabhāsaṁ, I bow especially to you, palomino-colored, shining over the earth:

Tayājja guttā viharemu divasaṁ. guarded by you let us live out this day.

Ye brāhmaṇā vedagū sabbadhamme, Those brahmins who are knowledge-masters with respect to all things,

Te me namo te ca maṁ pālayantu; I worship them, may they keep me safe.

Namatthu buddhānaṁ namatthu bodhiyā, Hail the Awakened Ones, hail Awakening!

Namo vimuttānaṁ namo vimuttiyā; Homage to the freed ones, homage to freedom!

Imaṁ so parittaṁ katvā, Having made this protection,

Moro carati esanā. the peacock wanders seeking.

Apetayaṁ cakkhumā ekarājā, This Clear-eyed One sets, the One King,

Harissavaṇṇo pathavippabhāso; palomino-colored, shining over the earth.

Taṁ taṁ namassāmi harissavaṇṇaṁ pathavippabhāsaṁ, I bow especially to you, palomino-colored, shining over the earth:

Tayājja guttā viharemu rattiṁ. guarded by you let us live out this night.

Ye brāhmaṇā vedagū sabbadhamme, Those brahmins who are knowledge-masters with respect to all things,

Te me namo te ca maṁ pālayantu; I worship them, may they keep me safe.

Namatthu buddhānaṁ namatthu bodhiyā, Hail the Awakened Ones, hail Awakening!

Namo vimuttānaṁ namo vimuttiyā; Homage to the freed ones, homage to freedom!

Imaṁ so parittaṁ katvā, Having made this protection,

Moro vāsamakappayī”ti. the peacock made his home.

Morajātakaṁ navamaṁ.
