Aṅguttara Nikāya 3.153 Numbered Discourses 3.153

15. Maṅgalavagga 15. Good Fortune

Catutthakhatasutta Broken (4th)

“Tīhi, bhikkhave …pe… “When a foolish, incompetent untrue person has three qualities they keep themselves broken and damaged. …

asucinā kāyakammena, asucinā vacīkammena, asucinā manokammena …pe…. Impure deeds by way of body, speech, and mind. …

Tīhi, bhikkhave …pe… When an astute, competent true person has three qualities they keep themselves intact and unscathed. …

sucinā kāyakammena, sucinā vacīkammena, sucinā manokammena—Pure deeds by way of body, speech, and mind. …”

Imehi kho, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato paṇḍito viyatto sappuriso akkhataṁ anupahataṁ attānaṁ pariharati, anavajjo ca hoti ananuvajjo ca viññūnaṁ, bahuñca puññaṁ pasavatī”ti.

Aṭṭhamaṁ.
