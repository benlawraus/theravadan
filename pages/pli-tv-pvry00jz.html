Kathinabheda

The sub-division on the robe-making procedure

1. Kathinaatthatādi

1. The robe-making procedure, etc.

Kassa kathinaṁ anatthataṁ?
Who has not participated in the robe-making procedure?

Kassa kathinaṁ atthataṁ?
Who has participated in the robe-making procedure?

Kinti kathinaṁ anatthataṁ?
How is the robe-making procedure not done?

Kinti kathinaṁ atthataṁ?
How is the robe-making procedure done?

Kassa kathinaṁ anatthatanti?
“Who has not participated in the robe-making procedure?”

Dvinnaṁ puggalānaṁ anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ—
Two kinds of people:

anatthārakassa ca ananumodakassa ca.
those who have not performed the robe-making procedure and those who have not expressed their appreciation.

Imesaṁ dvinnaṁ puggalānaṁ anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.

Kassa kathinaṁ atthatanti?
“Who has participated in the robe-making procedure?”

Dvinnaṁ puggalānaṁ atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ—
Two kinds of people:

atthārakassa ca anumodakassa ca.
those who have performed the robe-making procedure and those who have expressed their appreciation.

Imesaṁ dvinnaṁ puggalānaṁ atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.

Kinti kathinaṁ anatthatanti?
“How is the robe-making procedure not done?”

Catuvīsatiyā ākārehi anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
There are twenty-four ways in which the robe-making procedure has not been done:

na ullikhitamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
The robe-making procedure hasn’t been done merely by marking the cloth,

na dhovanamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by washing the cloth,

na cīvaravicāraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by planning the robe,

na chedanamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by cutting the cloth,

na bandhanamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by a tacking the cloth,

na ovaṭṭiyakaraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by sewing a seam,

na kaṇḍusakaraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by marking with a strip of cloth,

na daḷhīkammakaraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by strengthening,

na anuvātakaraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by adding a border lengthwise,

na paribhaṇḍakaraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by adding a border crosswise,

na ovaddheyyakaraṇamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by adding a patch,

na kambalamaddanamattena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
merely by partial dyeing;

na nimittakatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
nor has it been done if a monk has made an indication,

na parikathākatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if a monk has given a hint,

na kukkukatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if the robe has been borrowed,

na sannidhikatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if it has been stored,

na nissaggiyena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if it is to be relinquished,

na akappakatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if it hasn’t been marked,

na aññatra saṅghāṭiyā atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if it’s not an outer robe

na aññatra uttarāsaṅgena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
or an upper robe

na aññatra antaravāsakena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
or a sarong;

na aññatra pañcakena vā atirekapañcakena vā tadaheva sañchinnena samaṇḍalīkatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
nor has it been done if the robe hasn’t been made on that very day with five or more cut sections with panels,

na aññatra puggalassa atthārā atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ,
if the robe-making procedure isn’t done by an individual,

sammā ce atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, tañce nissīmaṭṭho anumodati.
or if the appreciation for the procedure is expressed by someone outside the monastic boundary.

Evampi anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.

Nimittakammaṁ nāma nimittaṁ karoti—
Making an indication: one makes an indication, thinking,

“iminā dussena kathinaṁ attharissāmī”ti.
“I will do the robe-making procedure with this cloth.”

Parikathā nāma parikathaṁ karoti—
Hinting: one gives a hint, thinking,

“imāya parikathāya kathinadussaṁ nibbattessāmī”ti.
“With this hint, I will make a cloth for the robe-making procedure appear.”

Kukkukataṁ nāma anādiyadānaṁ vuccati.
Borrowed: taking what has not been given is so called.

Sannidhi nāma dve sannidhiyo—
Stored: there are two kinds of storing:

karaṇasannidhi vā nicayasannidhi vā.
storing for the purpose of making and storing for the purpose of accumulation.

Nissaggiyaṁ nāma kayiramāne aruṇaṁ uṭṭhahati.
To be relinquished: dawn arrives while it is being made.

Imehi catuvīsatiyā ākārehi anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.

Kinti kathinaṁ atthatanti?
“How is the robe-making procedure done?”

Sattarasahi ākārehi atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.
The robe-making procedure is done through seventeen aspects:

Ahatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, ahatakappena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, pilotikāya atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, paṁsukūlena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, pāpaṇikena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, animittakatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, aparikathākatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, akukkukatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, asannidhikatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, anissaggiyena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, kappakatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, saṅghāṭiyā atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, uttarāsaṅgena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, antaravāsakena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, pañcakena vā atirekapañcakena vā tadaheva sañchinnena samaṇḍalīkatena atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, puggalassa atthārā atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, sammā ce atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, tañce sīmaṭṭho anumodati, evampi atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.
The robe-making procedure has been done if the cloth is brand new, if it’s nearly new, if it’s old, if it’s a rag, if it’s from a shop; it has been done if a monk hasn’t given a sign, if a monk hasn’t given a hint, if the robe hasn’t been borrowed, if it hasn’t been stored, if it’s not to be relinquished, if it has been marked, if it’s an outer robe or an upper robe or a sarong; it has been done if the robe has been made on that very day with five or more cut sections with panels, if the robe-making procedure is done by an individual, and if the appreciation for the procedure is expressed by someone inside the monastic boundary.

Imehi sattarasahi ākārehi atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.

Saha kathinassa atthārā kati dhammā jāyanti?
How many things are produced together with the doing of the robe-making procedure?

Saha kathinassa atthārā pannarasa dhammā jāyanti—
Fifteen things:

aṭṭha mātikā, dve palibodhā, pañcānisaṁsā.
eight summaries, two obstacles, and five benefits.

Saha kathinassa atthārā ime pannarasa dhammā jāyanti.

2. Kathinaanantarapaccayādi
2. The immediately preceding condition for the robe-making procedure, etc.

Payogassa katame dhammā anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo, purejātapaccayena paccayo, pacchājātapaccayena paccayo, sahajātapaccayena paccayo?
Which things are a condition for effort—by being an immediately preceding condition, by being an immediately prior condition, by being a support condition, by being a decisive support condition, by being a pre-arising condition, by being a post-arising condition, by being a co-arising condition?

Pubbakaraṇassa katame dhammā anantarapaccayena paccayo …pe…
Which things are a condition for the prior duties—by being an immediately preceding condition …

paccuddhārassa katame dhammā …
Which things are a condition for relinquishment …

adhiṭṭhānassa katame dhammā …
Which things are a condition for determining …

atthārassa katame dhammā …
Which things are a condition for doing the robe-making procedure …

mātikānañca palibodhānañca katame dhammā …
Which things are a condition for the summaries and the obstacles …

vatthussa katame dhammā anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo, purejātapaccayena paccayo, pacchājātapaccayena paccayo, sahajātapaccayena paccayo?
Which things are a condition for the subject—by being an immediately preceding condition, by being an immediately prior condition, by being a support condition, by being a decisive support condition, by being a pre-arising condition, by being a post-arising condition, by being a co-arising condition?

Pubbakaraṇaṁ payogassa anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo.
The prior duties are an immediately preceding condition for effort, and an immediately prior condition, a support condition, and a decisive support condition.

Payogo pubbakaraṇassa purejātapaccayena paccayo.
Effort is a pre-arising condition for the prior duties.

Pubbakaraṇaṁ payogassa pacchājātapaccayena paccayo.
The prior duties are a post-arising condition for effort.

Pannarasa dhammā sahajātapaccayena paccayo.
The fifteen things are a co-arising condition.

Paccuddhāro pubbakaraṇassa anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo.
Relinquishment is an immediately preceding condition for the prior duties, and an immediately prior condition, a support condition, and a decisive support condition.

Pubbakaraṇaṁ paccuddhārassa purejātapaccayena paccayo.
The prior duties are a pre-arising condition for relinquishment.

Paccuddhāro pubbakaraṇassa pacchājātapaccayena paccayo.
Relinquishment is a pre-arising condition for the prior duties.

Pannarasa dhammā sahajātapaccayena paccayo.
The fifteen things are a co-arising condition.

Adhiṭṭhānaṁ paccuddhārassa anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo.
Determining is an immediately preceding condition for relinquishment, and an immediately prior condition, a support condition, and a decisive support condition.

Paccuddhāro adhiṭṭhānassa purejātapaccayena paccayo.
Relinquishment is a pre-arising condition for determining.

Adhiṭṭhānaṁ paccuddhārassa pacchājātapaccayena paccayo.
Determining is a pre-arising condition for relinquishment.

Pannarasa dhammā sahajātapaccayena paccayo.
The fifteen things are a co-arising condition.

Atthāro adhiṭṭhānassa anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo.
Doing the robe-making procedure is an immediately preceding condition for determining, and an immediately prior condition, a support condition, and a decisive support condition.

Adhiṭṭhānaṁ atthārassa purejātapaccayena paccayo.
Determining is a pre-arising condition for doing the robe-making procedure.

Atthāro adhiṭṭhānassa pacchājātapaccayena paccayo.
Doing the robe-making procedure is a pre-arising condition for determining.

Pannarasa dhammā sahajātapaccayena paccayo.
The fifteen things are a co-arising condition.

Mātikā ca palibodhā ca atthārassa anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo.
The summaries and the obstacles are an immediately preceding condition for doing the robe-making procedure, and an immediately prior condition, a support condition, and a decisive support condition.

Atthāro mātikānañca palibodhānañca purejātapaccayena paccayo.
Doing the robe-making procedure is a pre-arising condition for the summaries and the obstacles.

Mātikā ca palibodhā ca atthārassa pacchājātapaccayena paccayo.
The summaries and the obstacles are a pre-arising condition for doing the robe-making procedure.

Pannarasa dhammā sahajātapaccayena paccayo.
The fifteen things are a co-arising condition.

Āsā ca anāsā ca vatthussa anantarapaccayena paccayo, samanantarapaccayena paccayo, nissayapaccayena paccayo, upanissayapaccayena paccayo.
Expectation and non-expectation are an immediately preceding condition for the subject, and an immediately prior condition, a support condition, and a decisive support condition.

Vatthu āsānañca anāsānañca purejātapaccayena paccayo.
The subject is a pre-arising condition for expectation and non-expectation.

Āsā ca anāsā ca vatthussa pacchājātapaccayena paccayo.
Expectation and non-expectation are a pre-arising condition for the subject.

Pannarasa dhammā sahajātapaccayena paccayo.
The fifteen things are a co-arising condition.

3. Pubbakaraṇanidānādivibhāga
3. The details on the source of the prior duties, etc.

Pubbakaraṇaṁ kiṁnidānaṁ, kiṁsamudayaṁ, kiṁjātikaṁ, kiṁpabhavaṁ, kiṁsambhāraṁ, kiṁsamuṭṭhānaṁ?
What is the source, the origin, the birth, the arising, the production, the origination of the prior duties?

What is the source, the origin, the birth, the arising, the production, the origination of determining?

Atthāro kiṁnidāno, kiṁsamudayo, kiṁjātiko, kiṁpabhavo, kiṁsambhāro, kiṁsamuṭṭhāno?
What is the source, the origin, the birth, the arising, the production, the origination of doing the robe-making procedure?

Mātikā ca palibodhā ca kiṁnidānā, kiṁsamudayā, kiṁjātikā, kiṁpabhavā, kiṁsambhārā kiṁsamuṭṭhānā?
What is the source, the origin, the birth, the arising, the production, the origination of the summaries and the obstacles?

Āsā ca anāsā ca kiṁnidānā, kiṁsamudayā, kiṁjātikā, kiṁpabhavā, kiṁsambhārā, kiṁsamuṭṭhānā?
What is the source, the origin, the birth, the arising, the production, the origination of expectation and non-expectation?

Pubbakaraṇaṁ payoganidānaṁ, payogasamudayaṁ, payogajātikaṁ, payogapabhavaṁ, payogasambhāraṁ, payogasamuṭṭhānaṁ.
The prior duties have effort as their source, origin, birth, arising, production, and origination.

Paccuddhāro pubbakaraṇanidāno, pubbakaraṇasamudayo, pubbakaraṇajātiko, pubbakaraṇapabhavo, pubbakaraṇasambhāro, pubbakaraṇasamuṭṭhāno.

Adhiṭṭhānaṁ paccuddhāranidānaṁ, paccuddhārasamudayaṁ, paccuddhārajātikaṁ, paccuddhārapabhavaṁ, paccuddhārasambhāraṁ, paccuddhārasamuṭṭhānaṁ.
Determining has relinquishment as its source, origin, birth, arising, production, and origination.

Atthāro adhiṭṭhānanidāno, adhiṭṭhānasamudayo, adhiṭṭhānajātiko, adhiṭṭhānapabhavo, adhiṭṭhānasambhāro, adhiṭṭhānasamuṭṭhāno.
Doing the robe-making procedure has determining as its source, origin, birth, arising, production, and origination.

Mātikā ca palibodhā ca atthāranidānā, atthārasamudayā, atthārajātikā, atthārapabhavā, atthārasambhārā, atthārasamuṭṭhānā.
The summaries and the obstacles has doing the robe-making procedure as its source, origin, birth, arising, production, and origination.

Āsā ca anāsā ca vatthunidānā, vatthusamudayā, vatthujātikā, vatthupabhavā, vatthusambhārā, vatthusamuṭṭhānā.
Expectation and non-expectation have the summaries and the obstacles as their source, origin, birth, arising, production, and origination.

Pubbakaraṇaṁ katihi dhammehi saṅgahitaṁ?
How many things are the prior duties grouped with?

Pubbakaraṇaṁ sattahi dhammehi saṅgahitaṁ.
With seven things:

Dhovanena, vicāraṇena, chedanena, bandhanena, sibbanena, rajanena, kappakaraṇena—
washing, planning, cutting, tacking, sewing, dyeing, and making.

pubbakaraṇaṁ imehi sattahi dhammehi saṅgahitaṁ.

Paccuddhāro katihi dhammehi saṅgahito?
How many things is relinquishment grouped with?

Paccuddhāro tīhi dhammehi saṅgahito—
With three things:

saṅghāṭiyā, uttarāsaṅgena, antaravāsakena.
the outer robe, the upper robe, and the sarong.

Adhiṭṭhānaṁ katihi dhammehi saṅgahitaṁ?
How many things is determining grouped with?

Adhiṭṭhānaṁ tīhi dhammehi saṅgahitaṁ—
With three things:

saṅghāṭiyā, uttarāsaṅgena, antaravāsakena.
the outer robe, the upper robe, and the sarong.

Atthāro katihi dhammehi saṅgahito?
How many things is doing the robe-making procedure grouped with?

Atthāro ekena dhammena saṅgahito—
With one thing:

vacībhedena.
breaking into speech.

Kathinassa kati mūlāni, kati vatthūni, kati bhūmiyo?
How many roots does the robe-making procedure have, how many bases, and how many grounds?

Kathinassa ekaṁ mūlaṁ—
The robe-making procedure has one root:

saṅgho;
the Sangha.

tīṇi vatthūni—
It has three subjects:

saṅghāṭi, uttarāsaṅgo, antaravāsako;
the outer robe, the upper robe, and the sarong.

cha bhūmiyo—
It has six grounds:

khomaṁ, kappāsikaṁ, koseyyaṁ, kambalaṁ, sāṇaṁ, bhaṅgaṁ.
linen, cotton, silk, wool, sunn hemp, and hemp.

Kathinassa ko ādi, kiṁ majjhe, kiṁ pariyosānaṁ?
What is the beginning, the middle, and the end of the robe-making procedure?

Kathinassa pubbakaraṇaṁ ādi, kriyā majjhe, atthāro pariyosānaṁ.
The prior duties are the beginning, doing it is the middle, the robe-making procedure is the end.

Katihaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo abhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ?
When a person has how many qualities, are they unable to do the robe-making procedure?

Katihaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo bhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ?
When a person has how many qualities, are they able to do the robe-making procedure?

Aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo abhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ.
A person who has eight qualities is unable to do the robe-making procedure.

Aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo bhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ.
A person who has eight qualities is able to do the robe-making procedure.

Katamehi aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo abhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ?
What are the eight qualities of a person who is unable to do the robe-making procedure?

Pubbakaraṇaṁ na jānāti, paccuddhāraṁ na jānāti, adhiṭṭhānaṁ na jānāti, atthāraṁ na jānāti, mātikaṁ na jānāti, palibodhaṁ na jānāti, uddhāraṁ na jānāti, ānisaṁsaṁ na jānāti—
They do not know the prior duties, relinquishment, determining, the robe-making procedure, the summaries, the obstacles, the ending of the robe-making season, or the benefits.

imehi aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo abhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ.

Katamehi aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo bhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ?
What are the eight qualities of a person who is able to do the robe-making procedure?

Pubbakaraṇaṁ jānāti, paccuddhāraṁ jānāti, adhiṭṭhānaṁ jānāti, atthāraṁ jānāti, mātikaṁ jānāti, palibodhaṁ jānāti, uddhāraṁ jānāti, ānisaṁsaṁ jānāti—
They know the prior duties, relinquishment, determining, the robe-making procedure, the summaries, the obstacles, the ending of the robe-making season, and the benefits.

imehi aṭṭhahaṅgehi samannāgato puggalo bhabbo kathinaṁ attharituṁ.

Katinaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā na ruhanti?
For how many kinds of people is the robe-making procedure not effective?

Katinaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā ruhanti?
For how many kinds of people is the robe-making procedure effective?

Tiṇṇaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā na ruhanti.
It is not effective for three kinds of people.

Tiṇṇaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā ruhanti.
It is effective for three kinds of people.

Katamesaṁ tiṇṇaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā na ruhanti?
For which three kinds of people is it not effective?

Nissīmaṭṭho anumodati, anumodento na vācaṁ bhindati, vācaṁ bhindanto na paraṁ viññāpeti—
For one who expresses their appreciation outside the monastic boundary; for one who does not express their appreciation verbally; for one who expresses it verbally, but no one hears it.

imesaṁ tiṇṇaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā na ruhanti.

Katamesaṁ tiṇṇaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā ruhanti?
For which three kinds of people is it effective?

Sīmaṭṭho anumodati, anumodento vācaṁ bhindati, vācaṁ bhindanto paraṁ viññāpeti—
For one who expresses their appreciation within the monastic boundary; for one who expresses their appreciation verbally; for one who expresses it verbally and another hears it.

imesaṁ tiṇṇaṁ puggalānaṁ kathinatthārā ruhanti.

Kati kathinatthārā na ruhanti?
How many kinds of robe-making procedures are invalid?

Kati kathinatthārā ruhanti?
How many kinds of robe-making procedures are valid?

Tayo kathinatthārā na ruhanti.
Three kinds of robe-making procedures are invalid.

Tayo kathinatthārā ruhanti.
Three kinds of robe-making procedures are valid.

Katame tayo kathinatthārā na ruhanti?
What are the three kinds of robe-making procedures that are invalid?

Vatthuvipannañceva hoti, kālavipannañca, karaṇavipannañca—
The subject fails; the timing fails; the making fails.

ime tayo kathinatthārā na ruhanti.

Katame tayo kathinatthārā ruhanti?
What are the three kinds of robe-making procedures that are valid?

Vatthusampannañceva hoti, kālasampannañca, karaṇasampannañca—
The subject succeeds; the timing succeeds; the making succeeds.

ime tayo kathinatthārā ruhanti.

4. Kathinādijānitabbavibhāga
4. The details on what is to be known about the robe-making procedure, etc.

Kathinaṁ jānitabbaṁ, kathinatthāro jānitabbo, kathinassa atthāramāso jānitabbo, kathinassa atthāravipatti jānitabbā, kathinassa atthārasampatti jānitabbā, nimittakammaṁ jānitabbaṁ, parikathā jānitabbā, kukkukataṁ jānitabbaṁ, sannidhi jānitabbā, nissaggiyaṁ jānitabbaṁ.
The robe-making ceremony is to be known; the robe-making procedure is to be known; the month of the robe-making procedure is to be known; failure of the robe-making procedure is to be known; success of the robe-making procedure is to be known; the giving of an indication is to be known; a hint is to be known; borrowing is to be known; storing is to be known; relinquishment is to be known.

Kathinaṁ jānitabbanti
“The robe-making ceremony is to be known”:

tesaññeva dhammānaṁ saṅgaho samavāyo nāmaṁ nāmakammaṁ nāmadheyyaṁ nirutti byañjanaṁ abhilāpo yadidaṁ kathinanti.
the robe-making ceremony is the grouping and coming together of those things, their name, appellation, label, expression, wording, designation.

Kathinassa atthāramāso jānitabboti
“The month of the robe-making procedure is to be known”:

vassānassa pacchimo māso jānitabbo.
the last month of the rainy season.

Kathinassa atthāravipatti jānitabbāti
“Failure of the robe-making procedure is to be known”:

catuvīsatiyā ākārehi kathinassa atthāravipatti jānitabbā.
the robe-making procedure fails in twenty-four ways.

Kathinassa atthārasampatti jānitabbāti
“Success of the robe-making procedure is to be known”:

sattarasahi ākārehi kathinassa atthārasampatti jānitabbā.
The robe-making procedure is successful through seventeen aspects.

Nimittakammaṁ jānitabbanti
“The giving of an indication is to be known”:

nimittaṁ karoti iminā dussena kathinaṁ attharissāmīti.
one makes an indication, thinking, “I will do the robe-making procedure with this cloth.”

Parikathā jānitabbāti
“A hint is to be known”:

parikathaṁ karoti imāya parikathāya kathinadussaṁ nibbattessāmīti.
one gives a hint, thinking, “With this hint, I will make a cloth for the robe-making procedure appear.”

Kukkukataṁ jānitabbanti
“Borrowing is to be known”:

anādiyadānaṁ jānitabbaṁ.
taking what has not been given.

Sannidhi jānitabbāti
“Storing is to be known”:

dve sannidhiyo jānitabbā—karaṇasannidhi vā nicayasannidhi vā.
there is storing for the purpose of making and storing for the purpose of accumulation.

Nissaggiyaṁ jānitabbanti
“Relinquishment is to be known”:

kariyamāne aruṇaṁ uṭṭhahati.
dawn arrives while it is being made.

Kathinatthāro jānitabboti
“The robe-making procedure is to be known”:

sace saṅghassa kathinadussaṁ uppannaṁ hoti, saṅghena kathaṁ paṭipajjitabbaṁ, atthārakena kathaṁ paṭipajjitabbaṁ, anumodakena kathaṁ paṭipajjitabbaṁ.
If cloth has been given to the Sangha for the robe-making procedure, what should the Sangha do? What should the one who performs the robe-making procedure do? What should one who expresses their appreciation do?

Saṅghena ñattidutiyena kammena kathinatthārakassa bhikkhuno dātabbaṁ, tena kathinatthārakena bhikkhunā tadaheva dhovitvā vimajjitvā vicāretvā chinditvā sibbetvā rajitvā kappaṁ katvā kathinaṁ attharitabbaṁ.
The Sangha should give the cloth, through a legal procedure consisting of one motion and one announcement, to the monk who is performing the robe-making procedure. The monk who is performing the robe-making procedure should wash the cloth, iron it, plan it, cut it, sew it, dye it, mark it, and then do the robe-making procedure.

Sace saṅghāṭiyā kathinaṁ attharitukāmo hoti, porāṇikā saṅghāṭi paccuddharitabbā, navā saṅghāṭi adhiṭṭhātabbā.
If he wishes to do the robe-making procedure with an outer robe, he should relinquish his old outer robe and then determine the new outer robe.

Imāya saṅghāṭiyā kathinaṁ attharāmīti vācā bhinditabbā.
He should then say, “I do the robe-making procedure with this outer robe.”

Sace uttarāsaṅgena kathinaṁ attharitukāmo hoti, porāṇako uttarāsaṅgo paccuddharitabbo, navo uttarāsaṅgo adhiṭṭhātabbo.
If he wishes to do the robe-making procedure with an upper robe, he should relinquish his old outer robe and then determine the new outer robe.

Iminā uttarāsaṅgena kathinaṁ attharāmīti vācā bhinditabbā.
He should then say, “I do the robe-making procedure with this upper robe.”

Sace antaravāsakena kathinaṁ attharitukāmo hoti, porāṇako antaravāsako paccuddharitabbo, navo antaravāsako adhiṭṭhātabbo.
If he wishes to do the robe-making procedure with a sarong, he should relinquish his old outer robe and then determine the new outer robe.

Iminā antaravāsakena kathinaṁ attharāmīti vācā bhinditabbā.
He should then say, “I do the robe-making procedure with this sarong.”

Tena kathinatthārakena bhikkhunā saṅghaṁ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo—
After approaching the Sangha, that monk who is performing the robe-making procedure should put his upper robe over one shoulder, raise his joined palms, and say:

“atthataṁ, bhante, saṅghassa kathinaṁ, dhammiko kathinatthāro, anumodathā”ti.
“Venerable Sir, the Sangha’s robe-making procedure has been done. The robe-making procedure is legitimate. Please express your appreciation.”

Tehi anumodakehi bhikkhūhi ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo—
The monks who are expressing their appreciation should put their upper robe over one shoulder, raise their joined palms, and say:

“atthataṁ, āvuso, saṅghassa kathinaṁ, dhammiko kathinatthāro, anumodāmā”ti.
“The Sangha’s robe-making procedure has been done. The robe-making procedure is legitimate. We express our appreciation.”

Tena kathinatthārakena bhikkhunā sambahule bhikkhū upasaṅkamitvā ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassu vacanīyā—
After approaching several monks, that monk who is performing the robe-making procedure should put his upper robe over one shoulder, raise his joined palms, and say:

“atthataṁ, bhante, saṅghassa kathinaṁ, dhammiko kathinatthāro, anumodathā”ti.
“Venerable Sir, the Sangha’s robe-making procedure has been done. The robe-making procedure is legitimate. Please express your appreciation.”

Tehi anumodakehi bhikkhūhi ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo—
The monks who are expressing their appreciation should put their upper robe over one shoulder, raise their joined palms, and say:

“atthataṁ, āvuso, saṅghassa kathinaṁ, dhammiko kathinatthāro anumodāmā”ti.
“The Sangha’s robe-making procedure has been done. The robe-making procedure is legitimate. We express our appreciation.”

Tena kathinatthārakena bhikkhunā ekaṁ bhikkhuṁ upasaṅkamitvā ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo—
After approaching a single monk, that monk who is performing the robe-making procedure should put his upper robe over one shoulder, raise his joined palms, and say:

“atthataṁ, āvuso, saṅghassa kathinaṁ, dhammiko kathinatthāro, anumodāhī”ti.
“The Sangha’s robe-making procedure has been done. The robe-making procedure is legitimate. Please express your appreciation.”

Tena anumodakena bhikkhunā ekaṁsaṁ uttarāsaṅgaṁ karitvā añjaliṁ paggahetvā evamassa vacanīyo—
The monk who is expressing his appreciation should put his upper robe over one shoulder, raise his joined palms, and say:

“atthataṁ, āvuso, saṅghassa kathinaṁ, dhammiko kathinatthāro, anumodāmī”ti.
“The Sangha’s robe-making procedure has been done. The robe-making procedure is legitimate. I express my appreciation.”

5. Puggalassevakathinatthāra
5. The robe-making procedure for an individual

Saṅgho kathinaṁ attharati, gaṇo kathinaṁ attharati, puggalo kathinaṁ attharatīti?
“Does the Sangha do the robe-making ceremony; does a group do the robe-making ceremony; does an individual do the robe-making ceremony?”

Na saṅgho kathinaṁ attharati, na gaṇo kathinaṁ attharati, puggalo kathinaṁ attharatīti.
“The Sangha does not do the robe-making ceremony, a group does not do the robe-making ceremony, but an individual does the robe-making ceremony.”

Hañci na saṅgho kathinaṁ attharati, na gaṇo kathinaṁ attharati, puggalo kathinaṁ attharati.
If the Sangha does not do the robe-making ceremony, nor a group, but an individual does, then,

Saṅghassa anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, gaṇassa anatthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, puggalassa atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ.
the Sangha has not done the robe-making ceremony, a group has not done the robe-making ceremony, but an individual has done the robe-making ceremony.

Saṅgho pātimokkhaṁ uddisati gaṇo pātimokkhaṁ uddisati puggalo pātimokkhaṁ uddisatīti?
“Does the Sangha recite the Monastic Code; does a group recite the Monastic Code; does an individual recites the Monastic Code?”

Na saṅgho pātimokkhaṁ uddisati, na gaṇo pātimokkhaṁ uddisati, puggalo pātimokkhaṁ uddisatīti.
“The Sangha does not recite the Monastic Code, a group does not recite the Monastic Code, but an individual recites the Monastic Code.”

Hañci na saṅgho pātimokkhaṁ uddisati, na gaṇo pātimokkhaṁ uddisati, puggalo pātimokkhaṁ uddisati.
If the Sangha does not recite the Monastic Code, nor a group, but an individual does, then,

Saṅghassa anuddiṭṭhaṁ hoti pātimokkhaṁ, gaṇassa anuddiṭṭhaṁ hoti pātimokkhaṁ, puggalassa uddiṭṭhaṁ hoti pātimokkhaṁ.
the Sangha has not recited the Monastic Code, a group has not recited it, but an individual has.

Saṅghassa sāmaggiyā gaṇassa sāmaggiyā puggalassa uddesā saṅghassa uddiṭṭhaṁ hoti pātimokkhaṁ, gaṇassa uddiṭṭhaṁ hoti pātimokkhaṁ, puggalassa uddiṭṭhaṁ hoti pātimokkhaṁ.
“When an individual recites to a united Sangha, to a united group, then the Monastic Code has been recited by the Sangha, by the group, and by the individual.

Evameva na saṅgho kathinaṁ attharati, na gaṇo kathinaṁ attharati, puggalo kathinaṁ attharati.
It is in this way that the Sangha does not do the robe-making ceremony, nor a group, but an individual does.

Saṅghassa anumodanāya gaṇassa anumodanāya puggalassa atthārā saṅghassa atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, gaṇassa atthataṁ hoti kathinaṁ, puggalassa atthataṁ hoti kathinanti.
When an individual does the robe-making procedure, and the Sangha expresses its appreciation, the group expresses its appreciation, then the robe-making procedure has been done by the Sangha, by the group, and by the individual.”

Kati kathinuddhārā saṅghādhīnā?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season are dependent on the Sangha?

Kati kathinuddhārā puggalādhīnā?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season are dependent on an individual?

Kati kathinuddhārā neva saṅghādhīnā na puggalādhīnā?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season are dependent neither on the Sangha nor on an individual?

Eko kathinuddhāro saṅghādhīno—
One kind of ending of the robe-making season is dependent on the Sangha:

antarubbhāro.
when the robe-making season comes to an end midway.

Cattāro kathinuddhārā puggalādhīnā—
Four kinds of endings of the robe-making season are dependent on an individual:

pakkamanantiko, niṭṭhānantiko, sanniṭṭhānantiko, sīmātikkamanantiko.
when one departs from the monastery, when the robe is finished, when one makes a decision, and when one is outside the monastic boundary.

Cattāro kathinuddhārā neva saṅghādhīnā na puggalādhīnā—
Four kinds of endings of the robe-making season are dependent neither on the Sangha nor on an individual:

nāsanantiko, savanantiko, āsāvacchediko, sahubbhāro.
when the robe-cloth is lost, when he hears about the end of the robe-making season, when an expectation of more robe-cloth is thwarted, and when the robe-making season comes to an end together.

Kati kathinuddhārā antosīmāya uddhariyyanti?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season happen within the monastic boundary?

Kati kathinuddhārā bahisīmāya uddhariyyanti?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season happen outside the monastery boundary?

Kati kathinuddhārā siyā antosīmāya uddhariyyanti siyā bahisīmāya uddhariyyanti?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season may happen within or outside the monastic boundary?

Dve kathinuddhārā antosīmāya uddhariyyanti—
Two kinds of endings of the robe-making season happen within the monastic boundary:

antarubbhāro, sahubbhāro.
when the robe-making season comes to an end midway, and when the robe-making season comes to an end together.

Tayo kathinuddhārā bahisīmāya uddhariyyanti—
Three kinds of endings of the robe-making season happen outside the monastic boundary:

pakkamanantiko, savanantiko, sīmātikkamanantiko.
when one departs from the monastery, when one hears about the end of the robe-making season, and when one is outside the monastic boundary.

Cattāro kathinuddhārā siyā antosīmāya uddhariyyanti siyā bahisīmāya uddhariyyanti—
Four kinds of endings of the robe-making season may happen within or outside the monastic boundary?

niṭṭhānantiko, sanniṭṭhānantiko, nāsanantiko, āsāvacchediko.
when the robe is finished, when he makes a decision, when the robe-cloth is lost, and when an expectation of more robe-cloth is thwarted.

Kati kathinuddhārā ekuppādā ekanirodhā?
How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season arise together and end together?

How many kinds of endings of the robe-making season arise together and end separately?

Dve kathinuddhārā ekuppādā ekanirodhā—
“Two kinds of endings of the robe-making season arise together and end together:

Avasesā kathinuddhārā ekuppādā nānānirodhāti.
The rest of endings of the robe-making season arise together but end separately.”

Kathinabhedo niṭṭhito.
The sub-division on the robe-making procedure is finished.

Tassuddānaṁ
This is the summary:

Kassa kinti pannarasa,
“Who has, how, fifteen,

dhammā nidānahetu ca;
Things, source, and cause;

Paccayasaṅgahamūlā,
Condition, grouped with, roots,

ādi ca atthārapuggalā.
And beginning, persons who do the robe-making procedure.

Tiṇṇaṁ tayo jānitabbaṁ,
For three, three, to be known,

atthāraṁ uddesena ca;
The robe-making procedure, and with recitation;

Palibodhādhinā sīmāya,
Obstacles, dependent on, with boundary,

uppādanirodhena cāti.
And with arise and end.”
