Heartfelt Sayings 7.7

Papañcakhayasutta

The Ending of Proliferation

Evaṁ me sutaṁ—
So I have heard.

ekaṁ samayaṁ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṁ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme.
At one time the Buddha was staying near Sāvatthī in Jeta’s Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika’s monastery.

Tena kho pana samayena bhagavā attano papañcasaññāsaṅkhāpahānaṁ paccavekkhamāno nisinno hoti.
Now at that time the Buddha was sitting reviewing his own giving up of the judgments that emerge from the proliferation of perceptions.

Atha kho bhagavā attano papañcasaññāsaṅkhāpahānaṁ viditvā tāyaṁ velāyaṁ imaṁ udānaṁ udānesi:
Then, understanding his own giving up of the judgments that emerge from the proliferation of perceptions, on that occasion the Buddha expressed this heartfelt sentiment:

“Yassa papañcā ṭhiti ca natthi,
“There is no proliferation remaining in them,

Sandānaṁ palighañca vītivatto;
the reins and bar are escaped;

Taṁ nittaṇhaṁ muniṁ carantaṁ,
the sage who lives without craving

Nāvajānāti sadevakopi loko”ti.
is never scorned by the world with its gods.”

Sattamaṁ.
