5. Cittātherīgāthā

Verses of the Elder Cittā

Cittā

“Kiñcāpi khomhi kisikā,
“I was skin and bones,
Though I’m skinny,

gilānā bāḷhadubbalā;
sick, and very weak,
sick, and very feeble,

Daṇḍamolubbha gacchāmi,
but leaning on a stick,
I climb the mountain,

pabbataṁ abhirūhiya.
I climbed the mountain.
leaning on a staff.

Saṅghāṭiṁ nikkhipitvāna,
I laid down the outer robe,
Having laid down my outer robe,

pattakañca nikujjiya;
and overturned the bowl.
and overturned my bowl,

Sele khambhesimattānaṁ,
Settled on a stone,
propping myself against a rock,

tamokhandhaṁ padāliyā”ti.
I destroyed this mass of darkness.”
I shattered the mass of darkness.

… Cittā therī ….
The Elder Cittā
